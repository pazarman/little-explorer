# Learning Design Principles (Ages 2–5)

## The Learning Loop
1. **Cue**: Simple instruction with ONE clear objective (e.g., "Tap the red one").
2. **Attempt**: Immediate child action (no waiting for long animations).
3. **Feedback**: Specific and supportive (e.g., "Red! You found it!").
4. **Reinforcement**: Repeat the concept in a varied way.

## Adaptive Difficulty (The "Fiona" Way)
- **Start Conservative**: Tier 0 should be accessible to a 2-year-old.
- **Auto-Assist**: If the child makes 3 mistakes, simplify the interaction (de-escalate).
- ** EMA of Performance**: We use an Exponential Moving Average to track quality.

## The Hint Ladder (Non-Negotiable)
1. **1st Mistake**: Verbal cue (repeat instruction or give a hint).
2. **2nd Mistake**: Visual cue (highlight the target, make it wiggle).
3. **3rd Mistake**: Guided assist (dim non-targets or move the target closer).

## Voice Guidelines
- **Voice-First**: The game must be playable without reading any text.
- **Prosody**: Use warm, encouraging tones. Avoid robotic repetition.
- **Varied Praise**: Use `praise()` helper for a variety of success sounds.
